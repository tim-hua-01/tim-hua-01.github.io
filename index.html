Bio

    My current focus is helping humanity navigate transformative AI, with a focus on technical AI safety research. I am a MATS scholar with Neel Nanda and Sam Marks. I am also a visiting fellow at Constellation and work from Berkeley, CA. Some of my recent work are:

  • Steering Evaluation Aware Models to Act Like They Are Deployed
    Tim Hua*, Andrew Qin*, Samuel Marks♦︎, Neel Nanda♦︎
    We trained models to act differently in evaluation and deployment and show that activation steering can elicit the deployment behavior in evaluation even when simple prompts cannot. Our work was cited in the Claude Opus 4.5 system card.
    Under Review & Poster at NeurIPS Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop
  • AI-Induced Psychosis: A Shallow Investigation
    Tim Hua
    I ran simulated conversation between AIs and users experiencing psychosis and uncovered dangerously sycophantic behaviors from some models. This work has been covered by press outlets such as Futurism and Business Insider, as well as by bloggers like Zvi and Tyler Cowen.
  • Combining Cost-Constrained Runtime Monitors for AI Safety
    Tim Hua, James Baskerville, Mia Hopman*, Henri Lemoine*, Aryan Bhatt, Tyler Tracy
    Given a set of monitors with different costs and accuracies, how can we efficiently combine them into a single monitoring protocol? We develop a mathematical model for monitoring and show empirically that combining multiple monitors together can pareto improve over using a single one.
