Bio

    I am an Economics-Math double major at Middlebury college and a visiting undergraduate student at Harvard college. I will join Lyft as an Economist intern this summer.

    I'm currently working as a research assistant at the Brookings Institution under Professor Carol Graham. We are exploring connections between well-being and deaths of despair during the pandemic, and I am a coauthor on the upcoming paper on the topic. In the 2020-2021 academic school year. I worked as a Research Assistant to Professor Martin Abel on his project aimed at reducing the intention-behavior gap. I have also done a personal research project examining COVID-19 tweets by governors and health experts with Chris Kim, Zihan Zhang, and Professor Alex Lyford. Our paper was published by the Journal of Student Research. A pdf of our work is available here, and you can find a copy of all of our data here.

    You can find some of my own mini research projects, as well as a solo-authored writing sample, in the research tab.

    I spend a lot of my spare time playing chess (even though I'm not that good at the game). I also enjoy biking, art museums, and old books. I read everything from the QJE to The Economist to fan fiction and Twitter threads. I put things I am reading/have read and found interesting in the Interesting Reads tab. There's some fun stuff in the random tab as well. (Ever wondered how to succeed with brunettes? This US Navy training video has got your back.)

    This purpose of the website is to 1. Help me keep track of interesting things I've come across 2. A platform to share these interesting things (I find myself sharing the same few things to others all the time so to make it easier I just send them my website haha) 3. Show off a little since all the cool kids have a website nowadays I want one too.
